Panner Makhni
Ingredients: For the Paneer Marinade: 250 grams paneer (cubed) 1 cup plain yogurt 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder Salt to taste For the Gravy: 2 tablespoons butter 2 tablespoons oil 1 large onion (finely chopped) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 3-4 large tomatoes (pureed) or 1 cup canned tomato puree 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1 teaspoon garam masala 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) 1/2 cup cream 1 tablespoon sugar (optional) Salt to taste Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish Instructions: 1. Marinate the Paneer: In a bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Add the paneer cubes and coat them well with the marinade. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours in the refrigerator for better flavor). 2. Prepare the Gravy: Heat the butter and oil together in a large pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook the mixture until the oil starts to separate from the masala. This may take about 10-15 minutes. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and fenugreek leaves. Stir well and cook for a couple of minutes. Add a little water if the gravy is too thick, and simmer for 5-7 minutes to let the flavors meld together. 3. Cook the Paneer: Gently fold the marinated paneer cubes into the gravy. Cook on a low flame for 5-7 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors. Be careful not to overcook as paneer can become hard. Stir in the cream and mix well. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt and sugar if needed. 4. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
